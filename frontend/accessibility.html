Our commitment

zazenware aims to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA, across every page of the site. Accessibility is a design value, not a checkbox — we want anyone who wants to browse our art or place an order to be able to do so.

We treat accessibility issues as bugs. If you encounter something that doesn't work, please tell us; we will work to fix it.

How we work toward this

The site is built with the following accessibility principles:

  • Semantic HTML. Pages use proper landmarks (header, nav, main, footer), one h1 per page, and a sensible heading hierarchy.
  • Keyboard navigation. Everything you can do with a mouse, you can do with the keyboard. Tab order matches visual order; menus, dropdowns, and the cart drawer are operable via Tab, Enter, Escape, and arrow keys.
  • Visible focus. Every interactive control shows a clear focus outline when reached by keyboard.
  • Skip link. The first focusable element on every page is "Skip to main content", which jumps past the header navigation.
  • Text alternatives. Images that convey meaning have descriptive alt text. Decorative images use empty alt so screen readers skip them.
  • Colour contrast. Body text meets AA contrast ratios against its background in both the light and dark themes.
  • Colour is never the only indicator. Selected, error, and disabled states all use additional cues — text, icons, ARIA attributes — not colour alone.
  • Forms with labels. Every form field has an associated visible label, and validation errors are announced to screen readers via role="alert" or aria-live.
  • Predictable behaviour. The site does not auto-play media, does not auto-submit forms, and does not change context without user action.
  • Reduced motion. If your system has "reduce motion" set, decorative animations are minimised.
  • Responsive layout. Pages work at 320 px wide and up, and reflow without horizontal scrolling at standard zoom levels up to 200%.
  • Light and dark themes. The theme toggle is keyboard-accessible and your preference is remembered. We also respect your system's preferred colour scheme on first visit.

What we use to verify

Each release is checked against:

  • Automated audits (axe DevTools, Lighthouse accessibility).
  • Manual keyboard-only walkthroughs of every page and every interaction.
  • Screen reader spot checks (VoiceOver on macOS / iOS, NVDA on Windows).
  • Zoom and reflow checks up to 200%.

What we don't yet support

  • Languages other than English. The site is English-only at launch.
  • Server-side rendering of dynamic content. The product catalog, cart, and forms are rendered client-side via JavaScript. If JavaScript is disabled, the site will not function. This is a known trade-off we accept for the simplicity of the vanilla frontend.

Tell us if something doesn't work

Email contact@zazenware.com with the subject line "Accessibility" and describe what you ran into. If you can, include:

  • What you were trying to do.
  • What happened instead.
  • What browser, operating system, and assistive technology you were using.
  • The URL of the page where it happened.

We aim to acknowledge accessibility reports within 7 days and to fix or respond substantively within 30 days.

Formal complaints

If you live in Ontario and we have not resolved an accessibility issue to your satisfaction, you may file a complaint with the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act office ↗. Federally, you may file with the Accessibility Commissioner of Canada ↗.
